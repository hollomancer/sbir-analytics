## Bottom line

Hypersonics is a **mid-sized, DoD-dominated** SBIR domain. Leaders can use the cohort and agency facts below for program scoping. Outcome channels are **not yet computed** — do not infer procurement or commercialization performance from this brief alone.

For **813** Phase II awards (Method A keyword pack, 1.19% of the 68,077-award universe):

| Measure | Result | How to use it |
|---|---:|---|
| Method A cohort (keyword pack) | 813 awards, 355 firms, ~$968M | Primary defensible hypersonics count |
| Share of all Phase II awards | 1.19% | ~¼ of nanotech footprint; concentrated in defense |
| Method A ⊆ Method B (CET) | 98.1% containment | Method A is nearly nested in CET; B is upper envelope |
| DoD share of awards | 87.7% | Expect procurement-first measurement when channels land |
| Awards from 2023 onward | 255 (31%) | Right-censored for mature transition windows |
| FPDS / Form D / M&A / subaward channels | **Not computed** | Absent artifacts — not zero evidence |

**Policy interpretation.** Hypersonics reporting should **not** copy nanotech’s dual-pathway headline table. Prioritize **FPDS + contract recovery (WS1) and subawards (WS5a)** over private-capital channels when signals are restored.

## What this provisional brief includes vs defers

## Finding 1: Method A is the defensible “hypersonics” label

Bare `supersonic` and ungated TPS/Mach language inflated a prior pack to 886; requiring core co-occurrence yields **813** awards, all core-admitted.

| Metric | Value |
|--------|------:|
| Method A | 813 / 810 unique IDs |
| Method B (CET, includes supersonic) | 962 / 960 unique |
| Intersection | 795 unique |
| Jaccard | 0.815 |

**Policy interpretation.** Report **813 under Method A**. Treat CET **962** as an upper bound that still mixes adjacent aero unless spot-checked.

## Finding 2: Near-pure defense customer base

| Agency | Awards | Share | Phase II $ (M) |

| NASA | 87 | 10.7% | 54.4 |

**Policy interpretation.** FPDS-coded Phase III is a **floor with known DoD bias**, not a neutral scorecard. Cross-agency comparison to NSF-heavy domains is structurally misleading.

## Finding 3: Scale supports descriptive stats; rare events need care

355 unique firms; top 10 = **24%** of awards. Half the cohort is 2020s activity (post-2018 hypersonics emphasis). **68** awards (8.4%) lack UEI — WS2 name resolution will matter.

**Policy interpretation.** Dashboards need **pathway-specific columns** and **subaward as a first-class channel** (prime-supply outcomes invisible in Phase III flags).

## What policy leaders should take away (now)

1. **Use 813 (Method A)** as the hypersonics SBIR Phase II scope for policy discussion.
2. **Plan for procurement-first evidence** — not a single blended transition rate.
3. **Right-censor 2023–2025 awards** before judging program outcomes.
4. **Restore signal artifacts** before comparing to nanotech headline table.

## Language to avoid

- Avoid: “hypersonics SBIR transition rate TBD = poor performance.”  
  Use: “Outcome channels not yet computed; cohort definition is complete.”
- Avoid: “962 hypersonics awards.”  
  Use: “813 under Method A keyword pack; 962 under broader CET taxonomy.”
- Avoid: “no Phase III means failed transition.”  
  Use: “indeterminate until contract-level and subaward channels are applied.”

## Recommended publication structure (when channels land)

1. Executive summary + headline table (extend with WS1/WS5a/FPDS rows).
2. Procurement and subaward findings first; Form D / M&A as secondary floors.
3. Measurement limits — FPDS coding gaps, FY2008 USAspending floor, censoring.
4. Agency reporting standard — separate channel columns.
5. Technical appendix — full findings + methodology.

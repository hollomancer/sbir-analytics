# Proposed `[L#]` Additions — Recent SBIR/STTR Literature (L34–L46)

**Status:** Proposal / advisory. These entries are **not** yet merged into
[`docs/research-questions.md`](../../research-questions.md). Review the wording, then paste the
entries into the **Prior literature & benchmarks** section there (continuing the existing
numbering, which ends at L33).

**Provenance.** Selected from the 179-work literature map (`citation_gap_memo.md`,
`citation_additions_core.csv`) — the strongest SBIR-specific *core* works per policy area that
the current `[L#]` list does not cite. All author lists, years, venues, and DOIs were fetched
from OpenAlex (`openalex_get_work`) and not written from memory. Each entry ends with a
`(→ …)` note naming the existing `[L#]` slot it updates.

**Curation (13 of 14 candidates kept).** An earlier draft included a 14th entry — a master's
thesis on the Air Force Open Topic mechanism — which was **dropped**: L34 (the JPE paper) covers
the same reform with far more authority, and thesis-grade work does not belong beside the
peer-reviewed entries. The remaining 13 are renumbered L34–L46 with no gap.

**Two entries are not standard journal articles (kept deliberately):**
- **L35** (Link & van Hasselt, *Small Firms and U.S. Technology Policy*) is a **book**
  (Edward Elgar) — a synthesis/companion to L10/L12, not a research article.
- **L43** (Howell & Brown, *Cash Windfalls*) is an **NBER working paper**; OpenAlex shows no
  published version as of June 2026, so cite the WP (the list already cites WPs, e.g. L10).

**Formatting note.** The existing `[L#]` entries do not carry the `(→ slot)` trailer; that line
is an editorial aid for this review. Strip it when pasting into `research-questions.md`, or keep
it as a brief relevance gloss — your call.

### National security / DIB (Section A)

- **[L34]** Sabrina T Howell, Jason Rathje, John Van Reenen, & Jun Wong (2025). "Opening Up Military Innovation: Causal Effects of Reforms to US Defense Research." *Journal of Political Economy* 133(11), 3605–3651. Causal estimates of DoD research-funding reforms (Air Force open-topic SBIR); relevant to the A3 leverage and open-topic questions. Published version of the earlier NBER working paper. <https://doi.org/10.1086/737235>  
  *(→ updates the A-section DIB econometrics (currently only policy reports L16/L17/L28–L33))*
- **[L35]** Albert N. Link & Martijn van Hasselt (2023). "Small Firms and U.S. Technology Policy." Edward Elgar Publishing. Synthesizing monograph (book) on U.S. small-firm technology policy; companion to Link & Scott [L12]. <https://doi.org/10.4337/9781802205596>  
  *(→ extends L10/L12)*

  *(→ updates D-section (currently grey-lit L19/L20/L21))*

"Evaluating the tail of the distribution: the economic contributions of frequently awarded government R&D recipients." *Research Policy* 51(7), 104539–104539.
